# Rajasthan Travel Guide
Rajasthan is known as the "Land of Kings" and is famous for its majestic forts, palaces, and vibrant culture.
Jaipur, the capital city, is called the "Pink City" due to its distinctive terracotta-colored buildings. Key attractions include Hawa Mahal, Amber Fort, and City Palace.
Udaipur is known as the "City of Lakes" and is one of the most romantic cities in India. Lake Pichola and the City Palace are must-visit spots.
Jodhpur, the "Blue City", is famous for Mehrangarh Fort, one of the largest forts in India.

# Tamil Nadu Travel Guide
Tamil Nadu is home to some of India's most spectacular temples and a rich Dravidian culture.
Chennai, the capital, is known for Marina Beach (one of the longest urban beaches in the world), Kapaleeshwarar Temple, and Fort St. George.
Mahabalipuram (Mamallapuram) is a UNESCO World Heritage Site famous for its rock-cut temples and Shore Temple.
Ooty (Udhagamandalam) is a popular hill station in the Nilgiri Mountains, known for its tea gardens and the Nilgiri Mountain Railway (a UNESCO site).
Pondicherry (Puducherry) is a charming former French colony known for its colonial architecture, Auroville, and serene beaches.

# Himachal Pradesh Travel Guide
Himachal Pradesh is a northern Indian state known for its stunning Himalayan landscapes.
Manali is a popular hill station and adventure sports hub. Rohtang Pass, Solang Valley, and Hadimba Temple are top attractions.
Shimla, the former summer capital of British India, features the Ridge, Mall Road, and Christ Church.
Dharamshala is home to the Dalai Lama and the Tibetan government-in-exile. McLeod Ganj is a popular backpacker destination.
The best time to visit Himachal is from March to June for pleasant weather, and December to February for snowfall.

# Northeast India Travel Guide
Northeast India is a hidden gem known for its untouched natural beauty and diverse tribal cultures.
Meghalaya is home to Cherrapunji, one of the wettest places on Earth, and the famous living root bridges.
Sikkim offers stunning views of Kanchenjunga, the third highest peak in the world. Gangtok, Tsomgo Lake, and Nathula Pass are popular attractions.
Assam is known for Kaziranga National Park (home to the one-horned rhinoceros) and its world-famous tea plantations.

# Budget Travel Tips
For budget travelers in India, consider traveling by train (book through IRCTC) as it is the most cost-effective way to cover long distances.
Hostels and guesthouses can cost as low as 500-1000 INR per night in most cities.
Street food is both delicious and affordable — expect to pay 50-200 INR per meal at local stalls.
Always negotiate auto-rickshaw fares before starting the ride, or use ride-hailing apps like Ola or Uber.
The best months for budget travel are the shoulder seasons — September-October and February-March — when prices drop but weather remains pleasant.
