# General Travel Tips for India
When traveling in India, it is important to always carry some cash, as smaller vendors may not accept digital payments.
The best time to visit most parts of India is during the winter months, from November to March, when the weather is cool and dry.
In the summer months (April to June), the weather can be extremely hot, especially in the northern plains.
The monsoon season (July to September) brings heavy rains, which can disrupt travel plans but also makes the landscapes lush and green.

# Goa
Goa is famous for its beaches, vibrant nightlife, and Portuguese heritage. 
North Goa is known for its energetic parties and crowded beaches like Baga and Calangute.
South Goa is preferred for a quieter, more relaxing experience with pristine beaches like Palolem and Colva.
Dudhsagar Falls is a magnificent four-tiered waterfall located on the Mandovi River, best visited right after the monsoons.

# Kerala
Known as "God's Own Country", Kerala is famous for its backwaters, especially in Alleppey and Kumarakom.
Houseboat cruises are a must-do experience.
Wayanad is a beautiful hill station in Kerala known for its spice plantations, waterfalls, and wildlife sanctuaries.

# Visa Information
Most foreign nationals require a visa to enter India. The e-Visa facility is available for citizens of many countries and is the most convenient way to obtain a tourist visa.
Always keep your passport and visa details handy while traveling.
